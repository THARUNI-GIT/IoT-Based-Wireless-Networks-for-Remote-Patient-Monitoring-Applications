# IoT-Based-Wireless-Networks-for-Remote-Patient-Monitoring-Applications
Academic assignment on IoT-based wireless networks for remote patient monitoring applications, focusing on architectures, communication technologies, challenges, and future research directions.

This repository contains the academic assignment titled **“IoT-Based Wireless Networks for Remote Patient Monitoring Applications”**, prepared as part of a university coursework requirement. The assignment investigates how Internet of Things (IoT) technologies and wireless communication networks enable continuous, remote monitoring of patient health in modern healthcare systems.

The work is written using LaTeX and follows a formal academic structure, emphasizing originality, scholarly references, and compliance with institutional guidelines on plagiarism and content organization.

## Purpose of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to study the role of IoT-based wireless networks in supporting remote patient monitoring applications. It focuses on the architectural foundations, communication models, wireless technologies, and practical healthcare use cases that enable real-time health data collection and transmission. The assignment also analyzes technical challenges and discusses future research directions in this rapidly evolving domain.

## Content Overview

The assignment begins with an introduction to IoT-enabled remote patient monitoring and its significance in healthcare delivery. It then presents background concepts related to IoT systems and wireless networking in medical environments. Core architectures and communication approaches are discussed, followed by a comparative analysis of wireless technologies commonly used in healthcare IoT. Practical applications and real-world use cases are examined, along with key challenges such as security, scalability, and reliability. The assignment concludes by outlining emerging trends and future directions in IoT-based healthcare monitoring.
